Reminders

Handheld-sized devices

Media-queries are mobile-first; use the UIFlex variable for '-handheld' as default.

Typically your first media-query will be for tablet-sized devices, however there will be exceptions to this rule.

Collapse breakpoint

Usually around 800px

Typically refers to the point that a site's main navigation "collapses" to become concealed behind a clickable menu icon, or similar.

Use 'min-width: breakpoint-collapse' to separate styles which become active on larger devices.

Note: While the '-collapse' breakpoint and the '-laptop' breakpoint are often the same, don't rely on this. '-Collapse' should only ever appear on its own in a media query as a min-width.

Tablet-sized devices

Usually around 560px

@breakpoint-tablet is the lower limit of the middle-sized breakpoint.

Target tablet-sized devices with '(min-width:@breakpoint-tablet) AND (max-width: (@breakpoint-computer - 1px))'

Computer and Widescreen-sized devices

Usually around 800px or 1200px

@breakpoint-computer is the lower limit of larger devices.

Target larger devices with 'min-width:@breakpoint-computer'

Note: Don't use '-tablet' as a synonym for '-collapse'

Maximum width

Usually around 2000px

With the advent of 4K screens, we need to be careful that we don't allow a page's layout to be stretched too far.

Use 'max-width:@breakpoint-maximum' to constrain a page layout

Be careful: @breakpoint-tablet is the upper limit of the breakpoint.

Minimum width

Usually around 520px

Not so much a media-query breakpoint... it's more of a way to control the width of Panels and paragraphs.

Be careful: @breakpoint-minimum is the upper limit of the breakpoint.
